Learn to Protect Yourself Against a Phishing Email

How to Dectect a Phishing Email

  1. After you open an email, hover your mouse over what could be a link (this includes regular blue-highlighted links and images).
    A link will appear on the bottom left corner of your window. If it appears suspicious, don't click it because it is probably a phishing email.

  2. Spelling mistakes

  3. Vague salutation such as "Dear Customer"

  4. Urgernt and threatening language

  5. Suspicious signature. For example, signature has a suspicious email address

Still Not Sure If It's a Phishiing Email?

Don't give personal information.

For example, if you get an email from your an organization saying that you need to change your password. Don't click on any links on the email. Instead, open a new window, access your bank the same way you always do, and then change your passoword.

For example, as you can see on "Image 2", an email was received from IFTTT. Instead of clicking on the links, one must go the IFTTT official website by opening a new window. If you don't know the exact URL address, google the organization instead ("Image 3", and IFTTT's website will be one of the first results.
If you know that you have an account with IFTTT, then change your password this way. If you know you don't have an account with IFTTT, don't bother to open the email.

Another example, if you get an email saying that you won something, then see who the email is coming from. Next, google that person/company. If not much information comes up, then it is probably a scam.

Learn to Make a Strong and Memorable Password

Step One

Think of a memorable sentence

Example

The Flying Squirrel Is A Furry Animal


Step Two

Take the letter from the beginning of each word

Example

The Flying Squirrel Is A Furry Animal

Result: tfsiafa


Step Three

Take your favorite word(s) from the sentence and change it's correspoting letter(s) to Capital Letter(s)

Example

The Flying Squirrel Is A Furry Animal

Result: tFSiafa


Step Four

Add numbers and symbols.

Here I add $4 after my favorite words (Flying Squirrel) to make it easier to rember where the number and symbol goes.

The password will be more secure if the number and symbols are in the middle of the password and not at the end or beginning.

Remember to make your password long. The longer it is, the more secure it is.

Example

The Flying Squirrel $4 Is A Furry Animal

Result: tFS$4iafa

Final Password: tFS$4iafa
